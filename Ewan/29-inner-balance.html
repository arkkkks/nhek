"Inner Balance" by Mark Metry

A student swings between overworking and complete shutdown. The story follows a healthier rhythm that balances effort, rest, and emotional stability.

At the beginning of Inner Balance, the character feels pressure, confusion, or fear and is not sure how to move forward. The story starts by naming the emotion honestly instead of hiding it.

In the middle, real setbacks still happen. Instead of giving up, the character learns to pause before reacting, ask for support, and use small daily habits that make hard days more manageable.

These repeated choices build confidence and self-control, turning emotional chaos into clear next steps.

By the end, the story shows steady growth rather than instant perfection. The character becomes more emotionally aware, more resilient, and better prepared for future challenges.
