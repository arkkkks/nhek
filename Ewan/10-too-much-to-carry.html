Too Much to Carry

Short story summary based on the cover title.

"Too Much to Carry" by Claire A. B. Freeland, PhD and Jacqueline B. Toner, PhD

School, family, and personal pressure pile up until one student feels crushed. The story teaches that sharing burdens and setting limits is not weakness but wisdom.

At the beginning of Too Much to Carry, the character feels pressure, confusion, or fear and is not sure how to move forward. The story starts by naming the emotion honestly instead of hiding it.

In the middle, real setbacks still happen. Instead of giving up, the character learns to pause before reacting, ask for support, and use small daily habits that make hard days more manageable.

These repeated choices build confidence and self-control, turning emotional chaos into clear next steps.

By the end, the story shows steady growth rather than instant perfection. The character becomes more emotionally aware, more resilient, and better prepared for future challenges.

Emotion focus: Stressed. Author listed for this copy: Claire A. B. Freeland, PhD and Jacqueline B. Toner, PhD.
